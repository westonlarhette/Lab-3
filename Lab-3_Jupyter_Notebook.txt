Python has different built in data types: integers, floating point, strings, etc

    Strings:
        .upper() - converts all characters to uppercase
        .lower() - converts all characters to lowercase
        .split(): breaks up strings into substrings
        
    Boolean variables - true or false
    
    Complex numbers: use j, .real gives real part, .imag gives imaginary part
    
    Sets: unordered collections of unique iterms
    Tuples: ordered, unchanging collection of items (used for storing data)
    Dictionaries: unordered collections of key-value pairs (e.g. pairing name or age to the ouput for a given person)
